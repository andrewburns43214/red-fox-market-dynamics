Pattern Definitions (v4)
INITIATED
Book moved before clear public pressure. Early information signal.
FOLLOW
Book moved with pressure. Market and public are aligned.
FADE
Book moved against pressure. Public is one way, price moved the other.
FREEZE RESISTANCE
Heavy pressure held in place by the book. The signal belongs to the opposite side.
FREEZE BALANCED
Pressure exists, but the hold looks balanced rather than resistant. Descriptive only.
FREEZE KEY NUMBER
Pressure sits on a structural key number. Usually not directional by itself.
FREEZE STALE
One book looks frozen relative to the broader market. This is a price-opportunity state, not broad conviction.
FREEZE WEAK
Pressure is too weak or too early for a no-move to carry meaning.
BUYBACK
The market moved, then reversed direction. Reversal or buyback behavior.
STALE
Pressure is visible while the displayed book price still looks stale.
NOISE
Mixed or weak market behavior. No clean reaction pattern.
NEUTRAL
No pattern detected. Score is driven by component math without a strong market reaction pattern.
Score Breakdown Components
Timing (0-3 pts)
Bonus or penalty based on when movement occurred. Late moves closer to game time carry more weight. EARLY, MID, and LATE timing buckets affect score.
Cross-Market (-5 to 5 pts)
Cross-market reinforcement or contradiction. Positive when spread, total, and moneyline signals align for the same game. Negative when they contradict.
